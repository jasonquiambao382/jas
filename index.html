Best Investment Apps- Our Recommended Stock Trading Platforms

Carrie Smith October 2, 2020
5 minute read
Advertising Disclosure This article/post contains references to products or services from one or more of our advertisers or partners. We may receive compensation when you click on links to those products or services

Here at Investor Junkie, our writers test many financial services. There are so many investment apps on the market that it can be quite confusing. Based on our research and experience, we want to recommend to you which ones we think are best. In most cases, we've used these stock trading apps ourselves.

Our Recommended Investment Apps

  • Best Overall Investment App – Ally Invest
  • Best Broker for Experienced Traders – TD Ameritrade
  • Best Personal Finance Software for Investing – Personal Capital
  • Best Investing App for Beginners – Betterment
  • Best Stock Trading App for Beginners – Public.com
  • Best Budgeting App – YNAB
  • Best Microsavings Service – Acorns
  • Best Investment Research – Morningstar
  • Best Paper Trading App – Webull
  • Best Active Trading App – Round
  • Best for Trading On The Go – ZacksTrade

In selecting these investment apps, we had the requirement that they must help either increase your investment returns, decrease your expenses, and improve your asset allocation.

We've created the following as a shortcut for your investing needs, but this doesn't mean we didn't fully test out these services. With most of the investment apps listed, we've done an in-depth review. So please also read the full review to find out why these services are on our recommendation list.

Not only do we share which ones are the best, but we explain why they're the top in their industry and what makes them so helpful. Of course, this is all from our perspective and personal experiences.

Best Overall Investment App – Ally Invest

Visit Ally Invest

Ally Invest charges zero commissions and has no hidden fees. A while ago, the service used to be called TradeKing. Now that it has merged with Ally Bank, Ally Invest has all of the same features as before, plus banking.

When Ally was TradeKing, it was ranked #1 by SmartMoney for customer service. I have found it to be among the most helpful customer service experiences I have had with a discount broker. Ally Invest also offers a comprehensive education center with helpful webinars and information on all the investments it provides.

Sign Up to Ally Invest

Best Broker for Experienced Traders – TD Ameritrade

Visit E*TRADE

TD Ameritrade has long been a major player among stock brokers. It offers a great selection of investment options, along with a variety of helpful investing tools. Plus, there's no required account minimum and the pricing is straightforward and relatively low. In addition, TD Ameritrade has brick-and-mortar locations across the country, so you can always stop in a branch office if you have a question.

Overall, this broker might be a bit complicated for new investors. But if you have some experience, you'll find it's hard to beat.

Start Investing With TD Ameritrade

Best Personal Finance Software for Investing – Personal Capital

Visit Personal Capital

If you are looking for a free web-based app that focuses on investing, then Personal Capital is the best option. Not only does this app allows you to track expenses, but it also helps review your investing plan.

Personal Capital gives you a holistic view of your finances, showing you the “big picture” of your financial life. With excellent customer service, robust security, and reporting capabilities that its competitors just can't beat, Personal Capital is an excellent tool in your financial arsenal. Also, Personal Capital's premium service offers financial advisors to guide you with your investment planning needs.

Sign up to Personal Capital

Best Investing App for Beginners – Betterment

Visit Betterment

If you are concerned about high fees, proper asset allocation, and whether a financial advisor has your best interest at heart, Betterment is the best solution to make investing easy. This service is excellent for investors who want investment guidance in an automated fashion, and at a much lower annual fee than traditional financial advisors.

Betterment focuses on your investing goals and hides the technical details on how to invest. This kind of focus is perfect for beginners who don't want to mess around with stock picking and instead want to invest for retirement.

Start Investing With Betterment

Best Stock Trading App for Beginners – Public.com

Download Public App

With zero commission fees and an easy-to-use investing app, Public.com is one of the best choices for first-time investors. You can easily pick stocks and buy slices of a share, instead of buying a whole share of a company. That means you can buy a portion of a share that might otherwise be too expensive. The interface is very simple and easy to navigate, which makes Public.com a great option if you want to learn the ins and outs of investing without navigating complicated charts and software.

Public.com also has a sharing feature, so you can see what others are investing in, without seeing the exact dollar amounts. It's a great way to explore trends and discover new companies that you may not have been aware of before.

Further Reading: Stock Trading Apps

Start Investing With Public.com

Best Budgeting App – YNAB

Visit YNAB

YNAB is an online app that does one thing — budgeting — and does it well. It is an easy-to-use personal finance management tool that links all your accounts in one place, tracking your spending over time. (However, if you are looking to track your investing, Personal Capital is a better bet.)

Previously, we recommended Quicken but have decided to recommend YNAB instead because Quicken had many synchronization issues with banks and a share of poor customer support issues over the years. Plus, the future of personal finance is online, not tied to a desktop.

Get Started With YNAB

Best Microsavings Service – Acorns

Visit Acorns

Typically, when you are just starting off saving, you don't have enough money. To get you on the path of not only saving but also investing, you need a service that makes it as painless as possible.

Acorns is that service. Once you link up your credit card, Acorns will round up each transaction to the nearest dollar and invest the difference. While not as ideal when compared to Betterment, Acorns is targeting primarily Millennials just starting with little money to save.

Start With Acorns

Best Investment Research – Morningstar

Morningstar Review

For those looking to research mutual funds, ETFs, or investing in stocks, Morningstar is a great resource! The website does not offer technical charts advising when you should buy and sell but instead provides you with information about proper asset allocation, low fee investment vehicles, and investments that generate consistent returns.

Every Morningstar investment review includes an analysis to give you the pros and cons. The service has a super-useful X-ray tool that ensures you aren't over-allocated in a specific sector or stock and a Cost Analyzer to help you choose one mutual fund or ETF over another. Free membership is available, but we believe the premium membership is well worth the cost, as you will likely make it up quickly in investment returns. Its Portfolio X-ray tool is second to none for usefulness and has some of the best investment research available. Read Our In-Depth Review.

Best Paper Trading App – Webull

Download Webull App

If you want to try out your investment strategies before you execute them, then Webull‘s app is a great choice. Also known as virtual trading, paper trading lets you get the hang of investing before you commit your hard-earned cash.

The service is very easy to use. All you need to do is download the Android or iOS app and set up an  account. From there, click on the paper trading icon. The virtual accounts come with $1 million to start trading with.  You can use this fake money to  buy or sell stocks in the simulated account that looks and feels just like what you would expect from a brokerage account.

Start Paper Trading With Webull

Best Active Trading App – Round

Round Investments review

For investors who want an actively managed portfolio but don’t have the time to do it themselves, Round is our top recommendation. With Round, your money is invested in a variety of ETFs, similar to a robo advisor. But unlike a robo advisor, your investments are actively managed and can include mutual funds and money market funds.

If you have questions, you can ask a human advisor through the in-app messaging. And best of all, you only pay fees when you make a profit. So when the value of your portfolio is down, you won’t pay anything. The only downside is that it’s only available on iOS. If you’re an android user, you will have to use their web version.

Sign Up for Round

Best for Trading On The Go – ZacksTrade

Visit Zacks Trade

You might have heard of Zacks Trade research, but they also have a great trading app. Called the Handy Trader, you can manage orders, monitor your portfolio and account balance, and more. You can also trade stocks, options, and other securities. 

ZachsTrade has some of the lowest trading fees in the field, although we definitely recommend them for more experienced users. They have an a la carte service so you only pay for the services that you need.

Start Trading with ZachsTrade
